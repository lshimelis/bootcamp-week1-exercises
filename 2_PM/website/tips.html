Tips and Tricks to Get You Started!

Camera Tips

  1. Larger Aperture to allow it to gather more light (which means more detail)
  2. ISO setting of 800 is a general Golidocks point, but you are encouraged to test other ISO settings
  3. Based on the ISO setting, a shutter speed between 10 and 30 seconds should be a good baseline
  4. Always choose manual focus over automatic so you have more flexibility to change the settings

General Tips

  1. Find a location free from light pollution to ensure that the photos are clear and you are able to locate celestial bodies
  2. Make sure to check the weather forecast ahead of time to optimize your shots (and not have to stand in bad weather)
  3. Plan ahead to get yoru timing right. For examplel, there are stars every night, but the Milky Way is most visible between March and October
